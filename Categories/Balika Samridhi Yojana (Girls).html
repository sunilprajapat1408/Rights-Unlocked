Balika Samridhi Yojana (BSY)

The Balika Samriddhi Yojana is a government scheme launched in 1997 to provide financial assistance to girl children from families falling in the Below the Poverty Line (BPL) category.

Features of Balika Samriddhi Yojana (BSY):
o BSY scheme provides financial assistance of Rs. 500 on the birth of a girl child
o The girl child further receives an annual scholarship in the range of Rs. 300 to Rs. 1000 until the completion of her education from 1st to 10th standard
o To be eligible for BSY, the girl child must fulfill the following conditions:
->Be born on or after August 15, 1997
->Belong to a BPL family
->Be enrolled in a school
o Only two daughters from a family are eligible to apply for this Central Government Scheme.
o The scheme helps to change the negative attitudes towards girl children in society.

Documents required:
• Birth Certificate of the Girl Child- issued by the hospital where the girl child was born or by the Government
• Address Proof of the parents or legal guardian– Passport, Driving License, Electricity or Telephone Bill, Voter ID Card, Ration Card or any other address proof issued by Govt. of India.
• Parents’ or Legal Guardian’s Identity Proof- Passport, PAN Card, Matriculation Certificate, Voter ID Card or any other certificate issued by Govt. of India which validates the identity of the girl child

Balika Samridhi Yojana (BSY)

The Balika Samriddhi Yojana (BSY) was unveiled by the Central Government in the year 1997. This was done under the blueprint for Women & Child Development for enabling empowerment of girl children in the country.

    Frequently Asked Questions...?

    The scheme is targeted at girl children born to families below the poverty line (BPL). The girl must be born after August 15, 1997, and the benefits are available for up to two girls per household.

    Under the scheme, a one-time grant of ₹500 is provided to the family upon the birth of a girl child. Additionally, annual scholarships are given to the girl for each year of schooling, ranging from ₹300 to ₹1,000 per year, from Class 1 to Class 10.

    The primary goal of the scheme is to change societal attitudes toward the girl child, encourage education, reduce the dropout rate, and delay marriage age. It aims to empower girls by offering financial incentives for their education and well-being.

    The scholarship amount is deposited in the girl’s bank account and can only be withdrawn by the girl when she reaches the age of 18, provided she is unmarried at that time. This is meant to encourage families to keep girls in school and delay their marriage.

    Yes, the accumulated scholarship funds can only be withdrawn once the girl turns 18 and is unmarried. This condition is in place to promote education and discourage child marriage.

    The scheme aims to improve the social status of the girl child, reduce gender inequality, promote girls' education, and ensure their financial security. It is part of broader efforts to address issues like the declining sex ratio, child marriage, and female literacy.
