Ujjwala Scheme

In May 2016, Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas (MOPNG), introduced the 'Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana' (PMUY) as a flagship scheme with an objective to make clean cooking fuel such as LPG available to the rural and deprived households which were otherwise using traditional cooking fuels such as firewood, coal, cow- ...

Eligibility criteria to avail connection under Ujjwala 2.0
1. Applicant ( woman only) must have attained 18 years of age.
2. There should not be any other LPG connection from any OMC in the same household.
3. Adult woman belonging to any of the following categories – SC, ST, Most Backward Classes (MBC), Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY), Tea and Ex- Tea Garden tribes, Forest Dwellers, People residing in Islands and River Islands, enlisted under SECC Households (AHL TIN) or any Poor Household as per 14-point declaration.

Necessary Document for Task

• Know Your Customer(KYC)

• Aadhaar Card of applicant as Proof of Identity and Proof of Address in case applicant is residing at the same address as mentioned in Aadhaar (not mandatory for Assam and Meghalaya).

• Ration Card issued by the State from which application is being made/ other State Govt. document certifying family composition/ Self-Declaration as per Annexure I (for migrant applicants)

• Aadhaar of beneficiary and adult family members appearing in document at Sl. 3.

• Bank Account Number and IFSC

• Supplementary KYC to support status of the family. Applicants may apply to any distributor of her choice either by submitting application at the distributor or by submitting a request through Online Portal.

Ujjwala scheme

In the first year of its launch, the connections distributed were 22 million against the target of 15 million. As of 23 October 2017, 30 million connections were distributed, 44% of which were given to families belonging to scheduled castes and scheduled tribes.The number crossed 58 million by December 2018.

    Frequently Asked Questions...?

    The scheme is targeted at women from BPL families. Eligibility is determined based on data from the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011. Applicants need to be adult women, belong to BPL households, and should not have an existing LPG connection in their household.

    Under the Ujjwala Scheme, beneficiaries receive a free LPG connection, financial assistance of ₹1,600 for the connection, and an option to purchase gas stoves and refills at subsidized rates. Some states also provide the first refill and a stove for free.

    Eligible women can apply for the scheme by submitting an application form at the nearest LPG distributor along with required documents such as proof of identity, residence, and BPL status. The forms are available both online and offline.

    Yes, under the Ujjwala Scheme, beneficiaries receive a subsidy on subsequent LPG cylinder refills. They can opt for a 5-kg or a 14.2-kg cylinder, and the subsidy amount is transferred directly to their bank accounts after purchase.

    No, the scheme is specifically for households that do not have any LPG connection. However, existing beneficiaries under PMUY can benefit from ongoing subsidies for refills.

    The primary objective of the Ujjwala Scheme is to reduce the reliance on traditional, polluting fuels like firewood, dung cakes, and coal, which pose health risks, particularly for women and children. The scheme aims to improve health conditions, empower rural women, and promote clean energy for cooking in India.
