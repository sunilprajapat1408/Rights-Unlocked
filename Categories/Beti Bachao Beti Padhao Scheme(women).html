Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme

Launched on 22nd January 2015 by the Prime Minister of India in Haryana, the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme ensures survival, protection and education of girl children. The scheme aims to address issues of the declining sex ratio over the past few years, create social awareness and enhance the efficiency of welfare services developed for girls.

The Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme is applicable to the whole country. However, to ensure maximum reach, this scheme is divided into 3 groups.

• Primary Group (young and married couples, pregnant mothers and parents)

• Secondary Groups (the youth of India, adolescents, in-laws, doctors, private hospitals, nursing homes, diagnostic centres)

• Tertiary Groups (general people of the country, religious leaders, voluntary organisations, frontline workers, officials, media and women SHGs Sector).

Necessary Document for Task

• Birth Certificate of the girl child

• Passport size photograph

• Proof of Identity of the Parents (Aadhaar Card, Ration Card, etc.)

• Proof of Address (Passport, utility bills like water, driving licence, telephone, electricity etc.)

    Frequently Asked Questions...?

    The scheme primarily targets all girl children in India, particularly in districts with low Child Sex Ratios. It focuses on raising awareness, improving welfare services for girls, and providing access to quality education across the country.

    The primary objectives are to prevent gender-biased sex-selective elimination, ensure the survival and protection of girls, and encourage the education and participation of girls in various fields.

    While the scheme itself does not provide direct financial incentives, it works in tandem with other government schemes such as Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana, which offers financial benefits to families for the education and well-being of their daughters.

    Individuals can contribute by raising awareness about the importance of gender equality, supporting girl child education, reporting cases of discrimination or violence against girls, and ensuring that their communities embrace and promote the welfare of girls in every aspect.

    The scheme promotes the importance of educating girls through awareness campaigns, improving school infrastructure, providing scholarships, and ensuring gender-sensitive educational policies to increase enrollment and retention rates for girls in schools.

    The scheme is implemented through collaboration between various government ministries including the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, and Ministry of Education. It is carried out at both national and district levels, focusing on regions with the lowest child sex ratios.
