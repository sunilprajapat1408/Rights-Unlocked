Crime Types and Legal Consequences

Categories CRIME CRIME Types and Legal Consequences

"Justice delayed is justice denied."

In any society, crime (usually defined as an action or omission or illicit activity that is punishable by law) exists, and so do the criminal laws. ‘Criminal law’ is a phrase containing just two words but having several acts enacted underneath it. The basic function of enacting any criminal law is to punish the guilty and provide justice to the injured party through legal procedures. Criminal law can be regarded as the body of laws that deal with matters of criminal wrongs, for instance, theft, murder, sexual abuse, threatening, etc., and is also accountable for conducting a trial for convicted offenders. Crime rates are increasing at a fast pace in India, which is why we need stringent bylaws to curb and deter such activities, thus keeping our society crime-free and making it healthy. Hence, in the following article, we will be looking at the basics of criminal law along with all the laws enacted for the purpose of safeguarding society from offenders.

Here, we delve into the intricate details of various crimes, the legal sections under which they are categorized, and the punishments meted out to those found guilty. This comprehensive table aims to educate and empower individuals by shedding light on the darker aspects of the legal system.

Types of Crimes and Punishments

Type of Crime Weapons Used Crime Sections Punishment Statement
Murder Firearms, Knives, Blunt objects, Poison IPC Section 302, 303 Death penalty or life imprisonment along with a fine
Attempt to Murder Firearms, Knives, Blunt objects, Poison IPC Section 307 Imprisonment up to 10 years, extendable to life, with or without a fine
Culpable Homicide Firearms, Knives, Blunt objects, Poison IPC Section 304 Life imprisonment or imprisonment up to 10 years, with or without a fine
Rape No specific weapon, but can include force IPC Section 376 Imprisonment of not less than 7 years, which may extend to life, and shall also be liable to a fine
Kidnapping No specific weapon IPC Section 363, 364, 365, 366 Imprisonment up to 7 years, extendable depending on the gravity, with or without a fine
Robbery Firearms, Knives, Blunt objects IPC Section 392, 394, 397 Imprisonment up to 10 years, extendable to life, with or without a fine
Dacoity Firearms, Knives, Blunt objects IPC Section 395, 396 Imprisonment for a term not less than 10 years, extendable to life
Theft No specific weapon IPC Section 378, 379 Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both
Burglary No specific weapon, tools for breaking in IPC Section 445, 446 Imprisonment up to 7 years, and fine
Cheating No specific weapon IPC Section 415, 417 Imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine, or both
Forgery No specific weapon IPC Section 463, 465 Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both
Counterfeiting No specific weapon IPC Section 489A, 489B, 489C Imprisonment for a term which may extend to life, and shall also be liable to fine
Assault Hands, Firearms, Knives, Blunt objects IPC Section 351, 352 Imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to Rs 500, or both
Criminal Intimidation Firearms, Threatening letters IPC Section 503, 506 Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both; severe intimidation can extend to 7 years
Sexual Harassment No specific weapon, but can include force IPC Section 354A Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or both
Trafficking of Persons No specific weapon IPC Section 370 Imprisonment for a term not less than 7 years, extendable to life, and shall also be liable to fine
Domestic Violence Hands, household objects, Firearms Domestic Violence Act, IPC Section 498A Imprisonment up to 3 years and also be liable to fine
Dowry Death No specific weapon IPC Section 304B Imprisonment of not less than 7 years but which may extend to imprisonment for life
Public Nuisance No specific weapon IPC Section 268, 290, 291 Fine, or imprisonment up to 6 months for continuance after injunction
Drug Trafficking No specific weapon NDPS Act Section 20, 21, 22 Imprisonment which may extend to 20 years, and fine up to Rs 2 lakhs
Bribery and Corruption No specific weapon Prevention of Corruption Act, IPC 171B Imprisonment up to 7 years, or fine, or both
Human Trafficking No specific weapon IPC Section 370, 370A Imprisonment for a term not less than 7 years, extendable to life, and shall also be liable to fine
Extortion Firearms, Threatening letters, Force IPC Section 383, 384 Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or with both
Criminal Breach of Trust No specific weapon IPC Section 405, 406 Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or with both
Insult with Intent to Provoke No specific weapon IPC Section 504 Imprisonment up to 2 years, or with fine, or with both
Rioting Blunt objects, Firearms, Knives, Stones IPC Section 147, 148 Imprisonment up to 2 years, or with fine, or with both
Arson Fire, Explosives IPC Section 435, 436 Imprisonment up to 7 years, extendable to life depending on the extent of damage, and also fine
Trespassing No specific weapon, but force may be used IPC Section 441, 447 Imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to Rs 500, or both
Adultery No specific weapon IPC Section 497 Decriminalized, but can be grounds for civil issues like divorce
Bigamy No specific weapon IPC Section 494 Imprisonment up to 7 years, and also be liable to fine
Obscenity No specific weapon IPC Section 292, 294 Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both
Cybercrime No specific weapon, but technology IT Act Section 66, 67 Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or with both; severe cases may extend to life imprisonment
Terrorism Firearms, Explosives, Chemical weapons UAPA, IPC Section 121, 122 Death penalty or life imprisonment along with a fine
Espionage No specific weapon, but tools for spying Official Secrets Act Imprisonment up to 14 years, extendable to life, and also fine
Defamation No specific weapon IPC Section 499, 500 Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both
Fraud No specific weapon IPC Section 420 Imprisonment up to 7 years, and also be liable to fine
Money Laundering No specific weapon PMLA Imprisonment not less than 3 years, extendable to 7 years, and fine
Prostitution No specific weapon IPC Section 372, 373, ITP Act Imprisonment up to 10 years, and also be liable to fine
Insider Trading No specific weapon SEBI Act Imprisonment up to 10 years, or fine, or both
Illegal Arms Trading Firearms, Explosives Arms Act Imprisonment up to 10 years, and also be liable to fine
Environmental Crime No specific weapon, but tools for pollution IPC Section 268, Environmental Acts Imprisonment up to 5 years, or fine, or both; severe cases may extend to life imprisonment
