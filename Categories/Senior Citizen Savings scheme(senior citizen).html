Senior Citizen Savings Scheme

The Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS) is primarily for the senior citizens of India. The scheme offers a regular stream of income with the highest safety and tax saving benefits. It is an apt choice of investment for those over 60 years of age.

Benefits of SCSS:

Here are a few reasons on why you should invest in SCSS:

● SCSS is an Indian government-sponsored investment scheme and hence is considered safe and most reliable.
● SCSS account includes a simple process and can be opened at any authorised bank or any post office in India.
● The account is transferable across India.
● The scheme offers a high interest rate on the deposit.
● Get an income tax deduction of up to Rs.1.5 lakh under Section 80C of the Indian Tax Act, 1961.
● The 5-year tenure of the account can be extended for another 3 years.

Documents required to open SCSS account:

● Two passport-size photographs
● Identity proof, such as a PAN card, Voter ID, Aadhaar card or passport.
● Proof of address, such as Aadhaar card or telephone bills.
● Proof of age, such as PAN card, Voter ID, birth certificate or senior citizen card.
● All the documents must be self-attested.

    Frequently Asked Questions...?

    Eligibility includes Indian citizens aged 60 years and above. Additionally, individuals aged 55 years or above who have opted for voluntary retirement can also open an account, provided they do so within one month of receiving their retirement benefits.

    The minimum investment amount is ₹1,000, and the maximum investment is ₹15 lakhs. Investments can be made in multiples of ₹1,000.

    The interest rate for the SCSS is determined by the government and is subject to change quarterly. Currently, it is around 8.2% per annum, paid out quarterly.

    The SCSS has a maturity period of 5 years, which can be extended for an additional 3 years after maturity if desired by the account holder.

    Yes, the interest earned is taxable under the Income Tax Act. However, the total investment is eligible for a deduction under Section 80C up to a limit of ₹1.5 lakhs per financial year.

    Yes, the account can be closed prematurely after one year, but a penalty may apply. If closed after one year but before three years, the penalty is 1.5% of the deposit amount; if closed after three years, the penalty is 1%.
